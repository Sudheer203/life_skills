## 1. Active Listening

**Video:** [Active Listening](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rzsVh8YwZEQ) (1:27 minutes)

### Steps/Strategies for Active Listening

1. **Focus fully** – Pay full attention to the person speaking.  
2. **Show you listen** – Nod, smile, or give small responses like "yes" or "I see."  
3. **Don’t interrupt** – Let them finish speaking before you reply.  
4. **Ask questions** – Clarify if something is unclear.  
5. **Summarize** – Repeat in your own words what you understood.  
6. **Reply wisely** – Respond thoughtfully to show understanding.

## 2. Reflective Listening

**Reference:** [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflective_listening)

Reflective listening is a type of **Active Listening** that focuses on **mirroring body language, emotions, and verifying messages**.  

### Where it is used

- Critical situations (e.g., Air Traffic Controllers and Pilots, military missions)  
- Business meetings – notes are taken and verified by participants  
- Personal relationships – to diffuse emotionally charged conversations  

### Application in Business/Software Context

1. **Take notes in meetings** – Capture all important points.  
2. **Share notes with stakeholders** – Client, manager, and team should verify.  
3. **Ensure team agreement** – Everyone should be on the same page.  
4. **Technical discussions** – Note down details to avoid mistakes during implementation.  

### Fisher's Model of Reflective Listening

**Key Points in Simple Words:**

1. **Listen carefully** – Pay close attention to the words and feelings of the speaker.  
2. **Show understanding** – Use body language or words to show you understand their emotions.  
3. **Mirror feelings** – Repeat or reflect what the speaker is feeling, not just the words.  
4. **Clarify** – Ask questions if something is unclear.  
5. **Confirm the message** – Make sure what you understood is what they meant.  
6. **Respond appropriately** – Reply in a way that shows you understood and respected their message.

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3: Obstacles in Your Listening Process

Some common obstacles are:  

- **Distractions** – Noise, phone, or multitasking.  
- **Prejudice or bias** – Judging the speaker before listening.  
- **Interrupting** – Talking before the speaker finishes.  
- **Daydreaming** – Mind wandering away from the conversation.  
- **Emotional block** – Strong emotions making it hard to focus.  

### Question 4: How to Improve Your Listening

Ways to improve listening:  

1. **Focus completely** – Remove distractions and pay attention.  
2. **Be patient** – Don’t interrupt, let the speaker finish.  
3. **Ask questions** – Clarify things you don’t understand.  
4. **Take notes** – Helps remember important points.  
5. **Practice empathy** – Try to understand feelings, not just words.  
6. **Summarize and confirm** – Repeat key points to ensure understanding.

### Types of Communication

1. **Passive Communication** – Avoids expressing feelings or needs.  
2. **Aggressive Communication** – Forceful, loud, or rude expression.  
3. **Passive-Aggressive Communication** – Sarcasm, gossip, taunts, silent treatment.  
4. **Assertive Communication** – Calm, honest, respectful expression of feelings and needs.  

### Question 5: When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- Example: When afraid of conflict or trying to avoid hurting someone’s feelings.  

### Question 6: When do you switch into Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- Example: When angry, stressed, or feel someone is not listening to you.  

### Question 7: When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

- Example: Using sarcasm, gossip, taunts, or giving silent treatment instead of being direct.  

### Question 8: How can you make your communication assertive?

**Steps to be more assertive (from videos and analysis):**  

1. **Think before you speak** – Stay calm and clear about your point.  
2. **Use “I” statements** – Example: “I feel… when …” instead of blaming.  
3. **Be confident, not rude** – Maintain a calm tone and steady body language.  
4. **Listen actively** – Show understanding of the other person’s view.  
5. **Set boundaries** – Say no politely when needed.  
6. **Practice regularly** – Use assertiveness in small situations to build confidence.
